Season One — شَبَح الريم · Ghost of Reem
01Logline

In Abu Dhabi, a city defined by order and silence, a quiet Emirati woman becomes the masked figure behind a calculated mall murder — as her life is slowly uncovered to reveal how emotional invisibility, a past humiliation, and unseen digital influence transform private resentment into public terror.

02Series Overview

Monsters of the Emirates is a psychological crime anthology drawn from real and rumored stories across the UAE. Each season explores a different "monster" — shaped by cultural memory, fear, and hidden social realities. Season One, Ghost of Reem, reimagines the 2014 Abu Dhabi case as a character-driven narrative: not a criminal to be caught, but a person to be understood.

Set in Abu Dhabi, the city functions as a central character — polished malls, quiet villas, wide highways, and controlled public spaces that appear stable yet conceal emotional silence. The story unfolds through CCTV footage, domestic routines, fragmented memories, and digital interactions. It asks not only what happened, but how someone can remain invisible until they become dangerous.

03Characters
01
Alaa Al Hashemi
30 — The Ghost · Central Character
Quiet, controlled, ordinary — until she isn't. Raised where obedience was virtue and silence was expected. Her arc maps invisibility into resentment, validation into radicalization. She believes she is gaining power. She is being shaped.
02
Mariam Al Nuaimi
35 — Investigator · Narrative Lens
She reads silence. The audience reconstructs Alaa's world through her. Both women are shaped by the same suppression — Mariam turns it into discipline. Alaa turns it into violence. They mirror each other before they ever meet.
03
Khalid Al Hashemi
40 — The Brother
Control as care. He notices every warning sign and chooses every easier explanation. He realizes too late: watching someone's behavior is not the same as knowing them.
04
Yousef
— The Voice · Digital Presence
He exists only through messages and voice notes. He gives Alaa recognition without responsibility. The twist: Yousef is not one person — he is a network, anonymous, possibly automated, entirely dangerous.
05
Ibolya Ryan
47 — The Victim · The Human Cost
Warm, grounded, ordinary. Shown through small moments: teaching, shopping, smiling at strangers. While the show explores Alaa's psychology, it never forgets the cost of violence. Ibolya represents a life interrupted — and keeps the series morally grounded.

05Story Engine — Plots & Subplots
Main Plot
The Unmasking
An investigation running in reverse — not hunting a suspect, but reconstructing a person. Mariam pieces together Alaa's interior world through CCTV, digital traces, and domestic silence.
Subplot A
The Radicalization
Alaa's online relationship with Yousef — how personal pain is given language, direction, and eventually a target. How influence operates without a single villain.
Subplot B
The Mirror
Mariam's investigation forces her to confront her own suppression. She and Alaa grew up in the same silence — separated only by the choices they made inside it.
Subplot C
The Family
Khalid's denial — and eventual guilt. How a household that maintained control at all costs created the conditions for collapse.

07Series Arc & Future Seasons

Monsters of the Emirates is designed as a long-form anthology. Each season explores a different "monster" shaped by Emirati society — drawing from true crime, urban legends, and rumor — while always asking the same question: what conditions create one?

Season Two
The Desert Ghost
Begins as a ghost story set in the desert outskirts. Reveals itself as a missing-person case shaped by denial, family secrecy, and the lengths people go to protect reputation over truth.
Season Three
The Haunted House
Rumors of possession in a family home uncover generational trauma and long-buried conflict. The supernatural becomes a lens for what families refuse to name.

Each season introduces new characters and settings while maintaining the anthology's thematic identity. The format is also expandable into a Ramadan extended run — each "monster's" story able to sustain 30 episodes of psychological unraveling.

08Tone & Visual Style / Comparisons

Slow, controlled, atmospheric. Tension is built through silence, repetition, and observation — not action. Familiar spaces become unsettling through stillness and subtle disruption. Abu Dhabi's ordered surfaces are the horror.

10MENASA Authenticity

"The UAE is integral to the story — not backdrop. Abu Dhabi's emphasis on order, privacy, and reputation shapes both the narrative and its characters."

Cultural dynamics — emotional restraint, family hierarchy, social expectation — are not texture. They are the engine. The series presents universal themes of fear, loneliness, and suppression through a specific Emirati lens. The MENASA identity cannot be removed from this story. It is the story.

11Why Me

"I experienced the fear surrounding Shabah Al Reem as something embedded within the community — not distant horror, but something that felt possible and close. I am not using the genre to hide a message. I am using it to explore one directly."
