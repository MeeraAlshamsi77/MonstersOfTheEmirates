FILE 002 —— SERIES CONCEPT

What does it mean
to be unseen?

وحوش الإمارات / Monsters of the Emirates is a psychological crime anthology series rooted in the UAE — in its architecture, its silence, its contradictions. Each season excavates a different kind of monster: not supernatural, not imported, but local. Born from suppression, rumor, social pressure, and the specific texture of Gulf memory.

Season One — شَبَح الريم / Ghost of Reem — is inspired by the 2014 Ghost of Reem case in Abu Dhabi. But the series is not a true-crime retelling. It asks a different question entirely: how does someone become invisible before becoming dangerous? The crime is almost incidental. The investigation is into everything that preceded it.

This is a show about what happens when cities are designed for safety but not for feeling. When presence is enforced but personhood is not. When the digital world offers the only mirror someone has ever been given.

FILE 003 —— PERSONS OF INTEREST

The People Inside the Frame

C-01
ACTIVE

Mariam Al Nuaimi

Emirati Investigator

Quiet, observant, and methodical. Mariam reads what people avoid saying — the pauses, the redirections, the things omitted from official reports. She does not chase evidence. She waits for it to reveal itself.

Arc Solving a case → confronting what the case reveals about the world she inhabits
C-02
SUBJECT

Alaa Al Hashemi

The Woman Behind the Mask

Emotionally restrained. Perfectly ordinary by every visible measure. She has learned to occupy space without being acknowledged in it — a skill she has refined until it became something else entirely.

Arc Invisibility → validation → manipulation → danger
C-03
WATCH

Khalid Al Hashemi

Alaa's Brother

Protective in the way that forecloses. He monitors Alaa's behavior with the attention of surveillance but without the understanding of intimacy. He watches and never sees.

Arc Guardian → complicit absence → reckoning
C-04
VICTIM

Ibolya Ryan

The Victim

Warm. Ordinary in the best sense of the word. Her life — her habits, her relationships, her small routines — exists in the series as a reminder of what was interrupted. She is not a prop. She is a life.

Function Anchors the audience in the human cost of invisibility rendered violent
C-05
UNKNOWN

Yousef

The Voice — Digital Presence

Not fully real. A presence assembled from messages, accounts, and carefully curated influence. Yousef says the things Alaa has never been allowed to hear. He is the first voice to make her feel found.

Twist Yousef is not one person — he is a network
FILE 004 —— SEASON ONE — EPISODE BREAKDOWN

Six Fragments of a Life Unseen

Episode 01 INCIDENT RECORDED

The Footage

The mall attack. CCTV. A masked figure, precise and unhurried. Investigator Mariam Al Nuaimi is assigned the case and realizes within hours: this was not impulsive. This was planned — meticulously, quietly, over time.

Episode 02 BACKGROUND

Invisible

We enter Alaa's life — before the mask. Her routines, her silences, her emotional erasure across years. The camera watches her the way the world never did. We see the preparation hiding in plain sight.

Episode 03 NEW SUBJECT

The Voice

Yousef appears. Messages, late at night. A digital presence that knows exactly what Alaa needed to hear. Mariam begins to trace the communications — and finds something she cannot immediately name.

Episode 04 ESCALATION

The Pattern

A second incident surfaces, connected to the first. The case expands. Mariam realizes she is not investigating one woman's breakdown — she is looking at something structural, something that has been operating quietly for a long time.

Episode 05 CLASSIFIED

Yousef

TWIST Yousef is not one person. He is a network — accounts, voices, and coordinated influence operating across multiple targets. The question shifts: was Alaa a participant or a victim?

Episode 06 FINAL

Unmasked

Alaa is arrested. The nation sees her face for the first time. But the influence does not end. Mariam closes the file and opens another. Somewhere, a new voice is beginning to speak — and someone is beginning to listen.

FILE 005 —— VISUAL LANGUAGE

The Aesthetics of Stillness

Tension built not from violence, but from what the frame refuses to show.

CCTV Gaze

The camera watches from above, from the corner, from the wrong angle. Footage feels observed, not directed. Grain, timestamp overlays, the coldness of surveillance infrastructure.

Mall Corridors

Bright, climate-controlled, beautiful — and profoundly indifferent. Marble floors that reflect everything. Wide corridors that make individuals small. Public space as the site of private collapse.

Empty Roads at Night

Abu Dhabi after midnight. Wide highways built for millions, traversed by one. Streetlight cones. The hum of air conditioning from dark windows. Vastness as loneliness.

Phone Glow

The blue light of a screen in a dark room. Notification sounds. The intimacy of a message received at 2am. The digital world rendered as the only world that sees her.

Reflections & Glass

Doubled images. A face in a shop window. A figure reflected in elevator doors. The show's subjects never appear straightforwardly — always mediated, always partially hidden.

Black Abaya Silhouette

A figure in the frame. Unidentifiable by design. Not menacing — ordinary. The horror of the series lives here: in how easily she disappeared into visibility.

In the tradition of ——
Mindhunter · The Killing · Mare of Easttown · Sharp Objects · True Detective S1
Distinct from all in its Gulf specificity, its Arabic cultural texture, and its centering of invisibility as a societal condition rather than an individual pathology.
FILE 006 —— THE ANTHOLOGY — FUTURE SEASONS

More Monsters.
More Emirates.

وحوش الإمارات is designed to run as long as the UAE has stories it has not yet told — which is to say, indefinitely. Each season introduces a new monster: a new case, a new protagonist, a new excavation of Emirati fear, memory, and social pressure.
